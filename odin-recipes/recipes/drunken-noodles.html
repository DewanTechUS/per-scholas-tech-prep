Chef John's Drunken Noodles (Pad Kee Mao)

Photo credit by Che John

Description

Spicy, sweet, and savory stir-fried rice noodles with aromatics, chiles, Thai basil, and (optional) chicken.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz dried rice noodles
  • 1/4 cup oyster sauce
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp white sugar
  • 2 tbsp cold water
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 cup shallots, thinly sliced
  • 6 tsp Thai bird’s eye chiles, thinly sliced (to taste)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 1/2 lb chicken thighs, strips (optional)
  • 1 lb Chinese broccoli, sliced
  • 4 scallions, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup fresh Thai basil leaves

Steps

  1. Soak rice noodles in hot water until flexible (not boiling water).
  2. Mix oyster sauce, soy sauce, fish sauce, maple syrup, sugar, and water.
  3. Heat oils in wok; stir-fry shallots, chiles, and garlic.
  4. Push aside; add chicken and sear briefly.
  5. Add Chinese broccoli stems, then leaves to wilt.
  6. Stir in sauce and scallions, bring to simmer.
  7. Add noodles and toss until coated and cooked through.
  8. Stir in Thai basil and adjust seasoning.

Notes

  • Use any noodle/pasta, but adjust cook times.
  • Swap chicken for pork, beef, shrimp, or veggies.
  • Regular basil works if Thai basil isn’t available.
